Quality Assurance and Software Testing

What is Quality Assurance?

QA stands for Quality Assurance. The name of the field is pretty good at explaining what the field is about. Quality assurance consists of a process that ensures quality through various testing methods. The end goal of a QA Analyst is to ensure the product meets the specification requirements as well as user/client needs.

Quality Assurance and Software Testing

Software Testing is QA in the field of Software. This entails testing software through methods such as white-box and black-box testing. Software Testers work closely with Software Engineers and Developers to create a final product with a minimal amount of bugs.

Infamous Bugs in History

Throughout the history of software, there have been many incidents where a bug left a company with billions of dollars in losses. One of the main goals of a software tester is to find bugs as early as possible in the Software Development Life Cycle. Finding bugs early avoids facing bigger issues later on. In 2007, due to a single bug within airport software, incorrect data was sent out to the US Border and Customs control network. This bug resulted in LAX having to shut down for eight hours. Also, 17,000 planes were grounded until the issue was resolved.
